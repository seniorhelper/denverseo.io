Technical SEO + GEO

Technical SEO Services Denver: The Foundation That Everything Else Depends On

There is no amount of great content or link building that overcomes a fundamentally broken technical foundation. We have audited hundreds of Denver business websites and the same technical failures appear repeatedly: pages that cannot be crawled because of misconfigured robots.txt files, duplicate content issues caused by URL parameter variations, Core Web Vitals scores that fail mobile users, schema markup that validates but contradicts the visible page content, and internal linking structures that leave high-value pages isolated from PageRank flow.

Our technical SEO audit for Denver businesses covers every layer:

  • ⚡Core Web Vitals (LCP, FID, CLS): Google uses these performance signals as ranking factors. We identify and fix the specific issues — usually render-blocking resources, unoptimized images, and layout shift triggers — that tank your scores.
  • 🔍Crawl Efficiency Analysis: We map how Googlebot and AI crawlers move through your site, identifying crawl traps, orphaned pages, and budget-wasting URL patterns that prevent your best content from being indexed.
  • 📋Schema Markup Implementation: We implement and validate structured data across 20+ schema types including LocalBusiness, FAQPage, HowTo, BreadcrumbList, Service, AggregateRating, Review, Speakable, and more — creating the entity signals AI systems rely on for citation.
  • 🕷Mobile Optimization: Over 70% of Denver searches happen on mobile devices. We audit and fix mobile rendering issues, tap target sizing, font legibility, and mobile page speed specifically for the devices your Denver customers actually use.
  • 🔗Internal Link Architecture: Strategic internal linking distributes PageRank efficiently, establishes topical relationships, and guides both users and search engine crawlers through your site's content hierarchy.
  • 📄Canonical Tag Management: Prevents duplicate content penalties by clearly signaling to Google which version of each URL is the authoritative one — critical for e-commerce sites and sites with URL parameters.

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) for Denver Businesses

GEO — Generative Engine Optimization — is the emerging discipline of optimizing your web presence specifically for AI language model citation. It is different from traditional SEO in important ways, though it builds on the same foundation of quality content and technical excellence.

Where traditional SEO asks "how do I rank on the first page of Google," GEO asks "how do I become the source that AI systems cite when someone asks a question in my category?" The answers are related but not identical. AI systems evaluate content for factual density, structural clarity, entity disambiguation, recency signals, and authoritativeness in ways that partially overlap with Google's ranking factors but also diverge significantly.

Our GEO work for Denver businesses includes building what we call an "entity profile" — a comprehensive set of structured content and linked data signals that establish your business as an unambiguous, authoritative entity in your industry and geography. This includes your Wikipedia-style entity page, Knowledge Graph optimization, structured data graph construction, and what we call "citation anchor content" — dense, factually rich pages on your website that AI systems prefer to cite when answering questions in your category.

A Denver HVAC company we worked with was invisible to AI search engines despite 12 years in business and solid Google rankings. After 60 days of GEO work — entity buildout, schema graph construction, and citation anchor content — their business began appearing in ChatGPT and Perplexity responses to "best HVAC companies Denver" queries. AI search now drives 18% of their inbound leads. That is revenue that would have gone to a competitor who had no GEO strategy.

Choosing the Right Denver SEO Services Provider

You will talk to a lot of Denver SEO agencies before you decide who to trust with your search visibility. Here are the five questions you should ask every one of them — and what the right answers look like.
